The 360-Degree View


These two charts simultaneously show 1) my full spectrum of skills and 2) how my proficiency in those skills have changed over time.

The first chart shows a tree of my skills by category. The size of each donut corresponds to my overall experience. Hence, data science is my biggest "chunk" of knowledge while web development plays a much smaller role. Statistics is a huge secondary skills but my knowledge of Machine Learning has been growing rapidly in the past two years.

The second chart is a bit tricky. The area between the two curves is the difference between that skill and the average of the larger categories. [If you look closely, you can see the color outline matches the graph on the left.] For example, take a look at data science > hybrid > elastic net, ridge, LASSO . There is a large area up until 2013. This shows that my average proficiency is hybrid methods are fairly linear with very little expertise in elastic net, ridge, and LASSO. However, in 2013, I gained quite a bit of experience using these methods for feature selection. Hence, those skill sets show exponential growth starting around that time.
